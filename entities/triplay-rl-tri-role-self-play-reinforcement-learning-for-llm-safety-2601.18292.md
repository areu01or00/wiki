# TriPlay-RL: Tri-Role Self-Play Reinforcement Learning for LLM Safety Alignment

## Overview
TriPlay-RL introduces a tri-role self-play framework for LLM safety alignment, decomposing the alignment problem into attacker, defender, and evaluator roles that co-evolve. This departs from the standard two-role (helpful/harmless) framing by introducing an explicit evaluator that provides structured safety feedback during training.

## Key Facts
- **Authors**: Tan, Zhewen; Yu, Wenhan; Si, Jianfeng + 9
- **Year**: 2026
- **arXiv**: [2601.18292](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.18292)

## Key Contributions
- First tri-role self-play framework (attacker + defender + evaluator) for LLM safety alignment
- Co-evolution of adversarial prompts and defensive strategies via structured evaluator feedback
- Addresses alignment tax by separating utility preservation (defender role) from safety improvement
- 10-author multi-institutional collaboration suggesting broad applicability of tri-role approach
