# Adaptive AI Delegation under Uncertainty: A Bayesian Governance Policy for Sequential Decision Authority

## Overview
Organizations increasingly deploy LLM and agentic AI systems to generate probabilistic assessments and candidate actions in high-consequence settings. This paper addresses a distinct managerial problem: how to dynamically allocate decision authority to AI-generated recommendations as evidence quality, uncertainty, and organizational objectives evolve over time. The authors propose a Bayesian governance policy that models the decision authority allocation as a sequential decision problem under uncertainty.

## Key Facts
- **Authors**: Dixon, Matthew Francis
- **Year**: 2026
- **arXiv**: [2606.29406](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.29406)

## Key Contributions
- Formalizes decision authority allocation as a sequential decision problem under uncertainty, distinct from standard prediction/classification tasks
- Proposes a Bayesian framework for updating decision authority thresholds as evidence quality and organizational risk tolerance evolve
- Demonstrates the policy on sequential decision settings where AI recommendations must be weighed against human oversight costs
- First paper in the wiki to explicitly frame AI governance as a dynamic, evidence-updating sequential decision problem rather than a static compliance exercise
