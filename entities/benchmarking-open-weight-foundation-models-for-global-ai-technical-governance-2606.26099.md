# Benchmarking Open-Weight Foundation Models for Global AI Technical Governance

## Overview
Large language models are increasingly deployed in AI governance analysis across national and international organisations. This paper finds that models produce significantly less accurate responses for countries underrepresented in training data — a geographic bias problem. The authors identify three methodological limitations in prior studies and propose an improved evaluation framework for assessing open-weight foundation models in governance contexts.

## Key Facts
- **Authors**: Jason Hung
- **Year**: 2026
- **arXiv**: [2606.26099](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26099)

## Key Contributions
- **Geographic bias in governance AI**: documents systematic accuracy disparities across countries in LLM-based governance analysis, tied to training data representation
- **Methodological critique**: identifies three limitations in prior work: reliance on proprietary systems, lack of weight transparency, and insufficient country-level disaggregation
- **Open-weight evaluation framework**: benchmarks diverse open-weight foundation models across governance tasks, disaggregated by country/region
- **Policy implications**: findings have direct implications for which models should be deployed for which governance contexts
