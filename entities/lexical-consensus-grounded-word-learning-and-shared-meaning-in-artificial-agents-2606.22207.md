# Lexical Consensus: Grounded Word Learning and Shared Meaning in Artificial Agents

## Overview
AI systems are typically evaluated through task performance and behavioral imitation, but these metrics leave open whether an artificial agent can acquire, stabilize, and use new lexical meanings from grounded experience. Lexical Consensus introduces a benchmark that tests whether agents can form shared meaning with human partners through iterative interaction — evaluating not just word-referent mapping but the social process of negotiating shared vocabulary for novel concepts.

## Key Facts
- **Authors**: Vera, Patricio M.
- **Year**: 2026
- **arXiv**: [2606.22207](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22207)

## Key Contributions
- **Lexical consensus benchmark**: First evaluation protocol that tests the social process of shared vocabulary formation — not just word learning but the iterative negotiation of meaning between agent and human
- **Grounded acquisition**: Agents must acquire new word meanings through embodied, situated interaction rather than from text corpora alone
- **Stabilization tracking**: Measures how stable learned lexical meanings remain under distributional shift in referent contexts
- **Human-agent alignment**: Evaluates whether agent's internalized word meanings align with human partners' intuitions — a novel form of semantic alignment distinct from value alignment
