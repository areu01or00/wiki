## Core Thesis (Neel Nanda)
The essence of an ideal paper is the narrative: a short, rigorous, evidence-based technical story with a takeaway readers care about.

**Two pillars:**
1. 1–3 specific concrete claims that build to a useful takeaway
2. Rigorous empirical evidence for why they are true

**TL;DR framework (one-glance):**
- **What?** — 1–3 specific novel claims in a cohesive theme
- **Why?** — rigorous empirical evidence supporting the claims
- **So what?** — motivation, impact, why the takeaway matters

**North star:** the reader must understand, remember the narrative, and believe the evidence supports it.

## Workflow Rules
- First step: compress research into claims before writing
- Iterative loop: abstract → bullet outline → intro → full draft → repeat
- Get feedback + reflect after each stage
- Spend comparable time on abstract / intro / figures / body (≈ `readers × time_to_read`)
- Quality > quantity of experiments — pick compelling ones
- Experiments explained at multiple altitudes: high-level intro → figures → technical body → appendix
- Aggressively red-team your own experimental evidence

## Tone
- **Inform, not persuade** — avoid overclaiming, acknowledge limitations
- **Precision, not obfuscation** — jargon only where it adds precision
- Simple language wherever possible
- Optimised for truth-seeking over conference acceptance

## Caveats (author)
- Author's primary domain: mechanistic interpretability
- Most advice generalises to ML broadly; some to other fields
- Personal opinion; not the only valid framework
