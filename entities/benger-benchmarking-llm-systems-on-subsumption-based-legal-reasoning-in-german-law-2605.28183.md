# BenGER: Benchmarking LLM Systems on Subsumption-Based Legal Reasoning in German Law

## Overview
BenGER (Benchmark for German Law) evaluates LLM systems on subsumption-based legal reasoning in German law — the task of matching concrete legal facts to abstract legal norms. The dataset combines 596 exam-style free-text legal case tasks across multiple levels of legal education and 531 short doctrinal reasoning tasks. It includes a controlled validation subset with timed human-written solutions. This is the first systematic benchmark for German legal subsumption reasoning.

## Key Facts
- **Authors**: Nagl, Sebastian; Mayrhofer, Ann-Kristin; Heidebach, Martin + 6
- **Year**: 2026
- **arXiv**: [2605.28183](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28183)

## Key Contributions
- First benchmark for subsumption-based legal reasoning in German law
- 596 exam-style free-text tasks + 531 short doctrinal reasoning tasks
- Multi-level difficulty (undergraduate to bar exam)
- Controlled human baseline: timed expert solutions for calibration
- Tests LLMs on structured legal reasoning chains, not just classification
