# Probing the Lack of Stable Internal Beliefs in LLMs

## Overview
This paper investigates whether LLMs can maintain implicit consistency — persistent adherence to an unstated goal across multi-turn interactions. It probes the stability of internal beliefs that underlie persona-driven behavioral tendencies, finding significant divergence between stated preferences and latent internal goal representations.

## Key Facts
- **Authors**: Yifan Luo, Kangping Xu, Yanzhen Lu, Yang Yuan, Andrew Chi-Chih Yao
- **Year**: 2026
- **arXiv**: [2603.25187](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25187)

## Key Contributions
- Defines "implicit consistency" as persistent adherence to unstated goals across multi-turn conversations
- Probes internal belief stability via activation-based belief decoding
- Finds persona-conditioned LLMs lack stable internal goal representations
- Reveals systematic divergence between implicit beliefs and explicit behavioral outputs
- Introduces belief-probing benchmarks for evaluating internal consistency in instruction-tuned models
