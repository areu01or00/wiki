# Are Multilingual Models Actually Improving? Isolating True Cross-Lingual Transfer

## Overview
Cross-lingual transfer is a model's ability to generalize capabilities from well-represented source languages to under-represented target languages. Existing measures of transfer strength conflate improvements in transfer with general improvements to accuracy in the source language. This paper advocates for an alternate metric that reliably captures true transfer strength, and provides an isolation methodology to determine whether multilingual models are genuinely improving their cross-lingual transfer capabilities or merely improving their source-language performance.
