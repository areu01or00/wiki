# Towards Physical Intuitions for Alignment Dynamics: A Case Study With Randomness Crystallization

## Overview
Proposes importing thermodynamic phase-transition theory into alignment research to explain how LLM behavior changes during post-training. Uses "Crystallization" (material phase transition) as an analogy for the three phases of alignment: (1) high-entropy pretraining liquid phase, (2) SFT nucleation phase collapsing onto seed distributions, (3) RL redistribution phase. Introduces intuitive metrics for verifying phase transitions and validates across random number generation tasks. First framework connecting physical thermodynamics to alignment training dynamics.

## Key Contributions
- First physical-science vocabulary (thermodynamic phase transitions) for alignment post-training dynamics
- Three-phase model: liquid (high-entropy pretrained) → nucleation (SFT collapse) → settling (RL redistribution)
- Crystallization case study: random number generation tasks decompose cleanly into 3 phase-transition stages
- Proposes measurable metrics to verify phase transitions in alignment (inaugurates new theoretical vocabulary)
- Argues alignment cannot fundamentally change certain structures (fundamental limitation of the settling phase)
- Inaugurates new theoretical framework — first of its kind in the wiki
