# Can AI Reason Like an Urban Planner? Benchmarking Large Language Models Against Professional Judgment

## Overview
UPBench introduces a domain-specific evaluation framework assessing LLM reasoning through a 4×5 matrix of four urban planning knowledge pillars and five cognitive levels adapted from Bloom's revised taxonomy. The paper systematically benchmarks whether LLMs can replicate professional planning knowledge versus requiring human judgment — revealing stark contrast between LLMs' statistical token generation and planners' experiential feedback loops.

## Key Facts
- **Authors**: Deng, Yijie; Zhu, He; Wang, Wen + 3
- **Year**: 2026
- **arXiv**: [2606.11678](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11678)

## Key Contributions
- UPBench framework: 4 knowledge pillars × 5 cognitive Bloom's taxonomy levels = 20-cell evaluation matrix
- First systematic domain-specific professional reasoning benchmark for LLMs beyond generic QA
- Demonstrates that LLMs lack situated engagement and reflection-in-action present in expert human planners
- Findings show LLM reasoning is statistically probable token sequences vs. experientially-grounded professional judgment
