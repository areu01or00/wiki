# Theory of Mind and Self-Attributions of Mentality are Dissociable in LLMs

## Overview
Safety fine-tuning in LLMs suppresses mind-attribution tendencies — including models asserting their own consciousness or experiencing emotions. This paper investigates whether suppressing mind-attribution degrades intimately related Theory of Mind (ToM) capabilities, finding that ToM and self-attributions of mentality are behaviorally and mechanistically dissociable. Safety ablation that removes self-consciousness does not impair the model's ability to model others' mental states.

## Key Contributions
- Demonstrates behavioral and mechanistic dissociation between ToM and self-attributions of mentality in LLMs
- Shows that safety fine-tuning that suppresses self-consciousness does not degrade ToM capabilities
- Provides representational similarity analysis showing dissociable attention patterns for self vs other mental state modeling
- Has implications for alignment: models can remain effective mental-state modelers while having self-attribution suppressed
