# Cognitive LLMs: Towards Integrating Cognitive Architectures and Large Language Models for Manufacturing Decision-making

## Overview
Cognitive LLMs resolves the dichotomy between the human-like yet constrained reasoning processes of Cognitive Architectures and the broad but often noisy inference behavior of LLMs. The paper investigates how Cognitive Architectures — developed for modeling internal mechanisms of human cognitive decision-making at a computational level — can inform LLMs with the process-level knowledge necessary for reliable reasoning in manufacturing decision-making contexts.

## Key Facts
- **Authors**: Siyu Wu, Alessandro Oltramari, Jonathan Francis, C. Lee Giles, Frank E. Ritter
- **Year**: 2024
- **arXiv**: [2408.09176](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.09176)

## Key Contributions
- First systematic investigation of integrating cognitive architectures (Soar/ACT-R) with LLMs for structured decision-making
- Addresses the noisy inference problem in LLMs by grounding outputs in cognitively motivated processing stages
- Evaluated in manufacturing decision-making — a domain requiring reliable, interpretable reasoning under constraints
- Proposed integration approach preserves the flexibility of LLMs while adding the structured reasoning guarantees of cognitive architectures
