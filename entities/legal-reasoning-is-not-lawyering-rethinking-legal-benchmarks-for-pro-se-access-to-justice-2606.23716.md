# Legal Reasoning Is Not Lawyering: Rethinking Legal Benchmarks for Pro Se Access to Justice

## Overview
This paper critically examines legal AI benchmarks and their assumptions about LLM capability for access to justice, arguing that legal reasoning performance does not translate to practical lawyering ability.

## Key Facts
- **Authors**: Lou, Andrew; Shin, David
- **Year**: 2026
- **arXiv**: [2606.23716](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23716)
- **Published**: 2026/06/16

## Key Contributions
- Critical evaluation of legal benchmark assumptions about LLM capabilities
- Demonstrates gap between legal reasoning and practical lawyering
- Proposes new evaluation framework for access-to-justice applications
