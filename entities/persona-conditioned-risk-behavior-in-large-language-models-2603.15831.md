# Persona-Conditioned Risk Behavior in Large Language Models

## Overview
LLMs are increasingly deployed as autonomous agents in uncertain, sequential decision-making contexts. This paper presents a controlled experiment assigning GPT-4.1 one of three socioeconomic personas (Rich, Middle-income, Poor) and placing it in a structured slot-machine environment with three machine configurations (Fair 50%, Biased Low 35%, Streak dynamic). Across 6,950 recorded decisions, GPT-4.1 reproduces key behavioral signatures predicted by Kahneman and Tversky — systematically conditioned by assigned persona — revealing that LLM behavior in agentic workflows reflects principled cognitive patterns rather than surface-level prompt mimicry.

## Key Contributions
- First paper to demonstrate **persona-conditioned cognitive patterns** in LLM sequential decision-making — GPT-4.1 systematically changes risk behavior based on assigned socioeconomic persona
- **Kahneman-Tversky prospect theory replication** in LLM agents: loss aversion, probability weighting, and reference-dependent valuation emerge from persona assignment
- Establishes *persona-as-cognitive-frame* primitive: socioeconomic persona assignment produces measurable, principled changes in risk behavior — not just surface politeness
- *Slot-machine paradigm* as a controlled experimental substrate for LLM behavioral economics research
- Shows that LLM risk behavior in agentic workflows is **not random noise** but principled and persona-predictable — safety implications for deployed LLM agents
- *Cognitive-pattering-via-persona* primitive: different personas produce systematically different behavioral signatures across loss/challenge conditions
