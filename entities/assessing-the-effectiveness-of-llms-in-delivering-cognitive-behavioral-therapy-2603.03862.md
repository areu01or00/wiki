# Assessing the Effectiveness of LLMs in Delivering Cognitive Behavioral Therapy

## Overview
This paper provides a systematic evaluation of how effectively LLMs can deliver Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), an evidence-based psychotherapy. As LLMs are increasingly used for mental health support, the paper benchmarks LLM-generated CBT interventions against established therapeutic principles, measuring fidelity to CBT protocols, quality of therapeutic alliance simulation, and clinical outcome proxies. This represents the first structured evaluation of LLMs against the specific mechanistic requirements of a clinical psychotherapy modality.

## Key Contributions
- First domain-specific clinical benchmark for LLM-delivered psychotherapy, operationalizing CBT fidelity metrics for automated evaluation
- Reveals that while LLMs can simulate therapeutic language effectively, fidelity to structured CBT techniques (cognitive restructuring, behavioral activation) varies significantly across models
- Establishes evaluation methodology for domain-specific LLM deployment in high-stakes contexts where mechanistic correctness matters beyond general language quality
