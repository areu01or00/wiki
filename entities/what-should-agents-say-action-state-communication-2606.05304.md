# What Should Agents Say? Action-state Communication for Efficient Multi-Agent Systems

## Overview
Multi-agent systems built on LLMs are typically organized around roles, pipelines, and turn schedules, while the content agents pass to one another is often unconstrained natural language. This free-form communication can rapidly inflate token usage. This paper analyzes five common inter-agent communication strategies across two MAS topologies, finding that no fixed strategy is universally optimal.

## Key Facts
- **arXiv**: [2606.05304](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05304)

## Key Contributions
- Analyzes five common inter-agent communication strategies across two MAS topologies
- Introduces action-state communication as a structured alternative to free-form natural language
- Demonstrates that optimal communication strategy is context-dependent (no universally optimal fixed strategy)
