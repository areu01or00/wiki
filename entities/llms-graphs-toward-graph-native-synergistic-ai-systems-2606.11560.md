# LLMs+Graphs: Toward Graph-Native, Synergistic AI Systems

## Overview
First tutorial-style survey synthesizing the algorithms, systems, and design principles driving the convergence of LLMs, graph data management, graph mining, graph ML, and agentic computation into next-generation *graph-native* AI systems. Identifies three complementary synergies (LLMs-augmented-by-graph-computation, bidirectional-LLM-KG-integration, graph-algorithm-augmented-AI-agents) and the new graph-ML capabilities that LLMs unlock.

## Key Facts
- **Authors**: Yin (2025) and collaborators (LLMs+Graphs)
- **Year**: 2026
- **arXiv**: [2606.11560](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11560)

## Key Contributions
- **Three complementary LLM-graph synergies formalized** — (i) LLMs augmented with graph computation for retrieval and reasoning, (ii) bidirectional integration where LLMs support KG construction/curation and KGs enforce semantic constraints and factual consistency, (iii) AI agents strengthened by graph algorithms for planning, decision making, and multi-step reasoning.
- **Graph-data-management and graph-ML capabilities unlocked by LLMs** — natural-language interfaces and hybrid LLM-graph-neural-network pipelines for graph data management and graph machine learning, identifying where LLMs introduce qualitatively new graph-data capabilities.
- **Unified design-principles synthesis** — offers data-science and data-mining researchers a unified perspective on integrating LLMs, graph data management, graph mining, graph ML, and agentic computation into graph-native AI systems.
- **Tutorial coverage of grounded inference** — emphasizes that graph-structured data underpins critical applications across social, biological, financial, transportation, web, and knowledge domains, making graph-aware inference a load-bearing primitive for context-rich reasoning.
