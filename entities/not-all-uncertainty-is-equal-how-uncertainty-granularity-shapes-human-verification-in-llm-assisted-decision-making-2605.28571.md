# Not All Uncertainty Is Equal: How Uncertainty Granularity Shapes Human Verification in LLM-Assisted Decision Making

## Overview
This paper investigates how the granularity of uncertainty displays affects human verification behavior in LLM-assisted decision making. Through user studies, the paper finds that coarse-grained uncertainty (e.g., "low/medium/high" confidence) leads to different trust calibration patterns than fine-grained probability displays, with implications for how to present LLM uncertainty to non-expert users.

## Key Facts
- **arXiv**: [2605.28571](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28571)
- **Year**: 2025
- **Theme**: UQ granularity + human-AI decision making

## Key Contributions
- Empirical study of uncertainty display granularity effects on human verification
- Shows that fine vs coarse uncertainty affect appropriate vs inappropriate trust
- Design implications for UQ in deployed LLM systems
