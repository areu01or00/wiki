# Four Types of LLM Reliance and Their Predictors Among Undergraduate Writers: A Mixed-Methods Study at a Minority-Serving R1 University

## Overview
This empirical study categorizes qualitatively distinct ways undergraduates rely on LLMs for academic writing — moving beyond frequency-based measures that inadvertently reward AI dependence rather than recognizing students' intellectual contribution. Uses mixed-methods approach at a minority-serving R1 university to identify four distinct reliance patterns and their predictors.

## Key Contributions
- First validated typology distinguishing qualitatively different LLM reliance patterns in academic writing
- Shows frequency-of-use measures reward dependence over genuine intellectual contribution
- Four distinct reliance types identified with different predictors
- Mixed-methods design at minority-serving institution captures diverse student populations
- Implications for AI policy in education and assessment design
