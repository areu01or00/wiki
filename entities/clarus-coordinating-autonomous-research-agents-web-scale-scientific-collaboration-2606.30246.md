# Clarus: Coordinating Autonomous Research Agents toward Web-Scale Scientific Collaboration

## Overview
Clarus addresses the fragmentation of autonomous research agents — most systems treat research as isolated assistant tasks or closed workflows, leaving scientific collaboration as a dark forest of disconnected tools. Clarus proposes a coordination infrastructure for web-scale scientific collaboration networks where multiple research agents share provenance, debate findings, and build on each other's work across open research ecosystems.

## Key Contributions
- Coordination infrastructure for multi-agent research networks at web scale
- Provenance tracking across distributed research agent workflows
- Open collaboration protocols enabling agents to build on peer findings
