# Are We Measuring Strategy or Phrasing? The Gap Between Surface- and Approach-Level Diversity in LLM Math Reasoning

## Overview
This paper identifies a critical gap in how LLM reasoning diversity is measured: existing diversity metrics capture surface-level phrasing variation but miss whether models are genuinely using different problem-solving approaches. The authors distinguish approach-level diversity (actual reasoning strategy differences) from surface-level diversity (phrasing differences) and show these two dimensions can diverge significantly in practice.

## Key Contributions
- Formal distinction between approach-level and surface-level diversity in LLM mathematical reasoning
- Demonstration that high surface diversity does not imply high approach diversity
- Analysis of how different reasoning strategies distribute across LLM architectures
- Implications for benchmark design and evaluation methodology
