# A Multi-Dataset Benchmark for Evaluating LLM Agents in Microservice Failure Diagnosis

## Overview
This paper introduces a large-scale benchmark for evaluating LLM-based agents in microservice failure diagnosis (AgentOps). The benchmark addresses a key gap in existing agent evaluation: most benchmarks score only final answers without assessing the systematic reasoning process that leads to those answers. The datasets cover multimodal observability data including traces, metrics, and logs, enabling assessment of both outcome quality and diagnostic reasoning quality in complex distributed systems.

## Key Contributions
- **Process-oriented agent benchmarking**: Evaluates both the final diagnostic answer AND the systematic reasoning process leading to the diagnosis
- **Multimodal observability data**: Covers traces, metrics, and logs from microservice environments — not just final outcomes
- **Large-scale datasets**: Multiple datasets covering diverse failure scenarios in microservice architectures
- **AgentOps evaluation**: Enables rigorous assessment of LLM agents operating in automated operations contexts
