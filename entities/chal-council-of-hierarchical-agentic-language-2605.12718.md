# CHAL: Council of Hierarchical Agentic Language

## Overview
Analyses structural limitations of multi-agent debate for LLM reasoning: debate induces martingale over belief trajectories, majority voting dominates observed gains, and LLMs exhibit confidence escalation rather than calibration across rounds. Argues genuine value of debate lies in defeasible domains rather than ground-truth tasks. Proposes hierarchical agentic council structure as an alternative.

## Key Facts
- **Authors**: Tommaso Giovannelli, Griffin D. Kent
- **Year**: 2025
- **arXiv**: [2605.12718](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12718)

## Key Contributions
- Structural critique of multi-agent debate: martingale belief trajectories, majority voting dominance, confidence escalation
- Argues debate's genuine value is in defeasible domains (not ground-truth tasks)
- Proposes hierarchical agentic council (CHAL) structure as alternative to flat debate
- First systematic analysis of multi-agent debate failure modes in the wiki
