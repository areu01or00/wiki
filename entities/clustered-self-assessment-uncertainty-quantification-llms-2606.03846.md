# Clustered Self-Assessment: A Simple yet Effective Method for Uncertainty Quantification in Large Language Models

## Overview
Clustered Self-Assessment addresses the problem of LLM responses appearing plausible while being factually incorrect. The method enables models to self-assess their uncertainty without external verification, providing users with explicit confidence estimates that complement the model's generated outputs.

## Key Contributions
- Simple clustering-based approach to self-assessment of LLM outputs
- Provides explicit uncertainty estimates to flag factually incorrect plausible-sounding responses
- No external verifier required — model self-assesses via clustered representations
- Orthogonal to semantic entropy and conformal prediction approaches
