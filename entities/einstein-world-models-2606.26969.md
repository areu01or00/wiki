# Einstein World Models

## Overview
This paper investigates whether LLM-based agents can reason about physical phenomena beyond what language alone can capture. The key insight is that physical intuitions — support relations, object permanence, causal spatiotemporal sequences — require structured representations analogous to human physical cognition. Einstein World Models introduces architectures and training signals that give LLMs the ability to build and reason with explicit physical world models, moving beyond purely language-mediated reasoning for physical scenarios.

## Key Facts
- **Authors**: Munachiso Samuel Nwadike, Zangir Iklassov et al.
- **Year**: 2026
- **arXiv**: [2606.26969](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26969)
- **Theme**: physical reasoning / world models / beyond-language representation

## Key Contributions
- **Physical world models beyond language**: Demonstrates that purely token-based LLM reasoning is insufficient for physical intuitions; introduces structured representations for physical phenomena
- **Architectures for physical representation**: Proposes mechanisms for building and reasoning with explicit physical world models in LLMs, analogous to how humans maintain naive physics
- **Training signals for physical reasoning**: Introduces training objectives that develop physical intuitions in LLMs beyond what is derivable from text corpora alone
- **Contrast with prior world model work**: Prior world model papers (Qwen-AgentWorld, In-Context World Modeling) are primarily language-mediated; Einstein World Models focuses on structured physical representation
