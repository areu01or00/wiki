# Think$^2$: Grounded Metacognitive Reasoning in Large Language Models

## Overview
Think$^2$ introduces a psychologically grounded metacognitive framework that operationalizes Ann Brown's regulatory cycle (Planning, Monitoring, and Evaluation) as a structured prompting architecture within a lightweight dual-process MetaController. The framework enables LLMs to reliably monitor, diagnose, and correct their own errors through adaptive effort allocation across reasoning and diagnostic benchmarks.

## Key Facts
- **Authors**: Abraham Paul Elenjical, Vivek Hruday Kavuri, Vasudeva Varma
- **Year**: 2026
- **arXiv**: [2602.18806](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.18806)

## Key Contributions
- Operationalizes Ann Brown's metacognitive regulatory cycle (Planning, Monitoring, Evaluation) as a prompting architecture
- Dual-process MetaController for adaptive effort allocation — switches between fast and slow processing based on uncertainty detection
- Evaluated on GSM8K, CRUXEval, MBPP, AIME, CorrectBench, and TruthfulQA
- First paper to ground metacognitive regulation theory directly into LLM prompting architecture
