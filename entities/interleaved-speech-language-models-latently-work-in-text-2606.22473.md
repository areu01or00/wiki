# Interleaved Speech Language Models Latently Work In Text

## Overview
Speech language models trained on interleaved speech-text sequences are shown to develop latent representations that function effectively in the text modality, even without explicit text generation during training. The work analyzes how speech and text modalities interact in the model latent space across different model families and sizes, revealing that speech-text interleaving improves speech-only capabilities while also developing text-like latent representations.

## Key Facts
- **arXiv**: [2606.22473](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22473)

## Key Contributions
- Demonstrates speech-text interleaving produces text-like latent representations without text generation
- Analysis across multiple model families and sizes of modality interaction in latent space
- Shows improvement on speech-only capabilities via interleaved training
- First systematic analysis of latent modality bridging in interleaved speech-text LMs
