## Updates
- [[kalm-reranker-v1-fast-not-late-interaction-compressed-2606.22807]] — KaLM-Reranker-V1: Fast but Not Late Interaction for Compressed Document Reranking: presents a fast-but-not-late-interaction (FBNL) reranker that decouples passage and query computation by using an encoder-decoder architecture — the encoder pre-encodes passages with Matryoshka embedding pooling while the decoder models the system instruction, user instruction, and query intent, with cross-attention capturing relevance — yielding deployment-flexible reranking that achieves SOTA on BEIR on par with industrial Qwen3-Reranker models and strong multilingual performance on MIRACL despite limited multilingual training data; Zhao, Xinping; Xu, Jiaxin; Dai, Ziqi; Zhang, Xin; Huang, Shouzheng; Tang, Danyu; Hu, Xinshuo; Zhang, Meishan; Hu, Baotian; Zhang, Min instantiate the family in three sizes (Nano / Small / Large at 0.27B / 1B / 4B activated parameters), making compressed-storage pre-encoded passage reranking practical at retrieval-system scale where the encoder-side computation dominates the deployment cost; 2026-06-22 (cs.CL, cs.IR); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (LLM reranker / retrieval-efficiency / encoder-decoder-decoupling theme). Complements the [[sproutrag-attention-guided-tree-search-with-progressive-embeddings-for-long-2606.18381]] (hierarchical-RAG sibling) and [[hakari-bench-a-lightweight-benchmark-for-comparing-retrieval-architectures-and-efficiency-settings-under-unified-conditions-2606.22778]] (retrieval-architecture-benchmark sibling) entries on this page by attacking the joint-encoding bottleneck that tightly couples query and passage computation in most existing rerankers, and surfacing Matryoshka passage encoding + decoder-side cross-attention as the architectural pattern that makes compressed-passage reranking practical.

- [[world-value-models-robotic-manipulation-2606.24742]] — World Value Models for Robotic Manipulation: argue that VLM-backboned value models lack the temporal modeling capabilities required for accurate value estimation because they are pretrained on static or temporally sparse visual observations, and marry world models with value estimation to construct a generalist World Value Model (WVM) that grounds current belief using historical context and plans over future outcomes — achieving SOTA Value-Order Correlation on standard benchmarks; Wang, Zhihao; Li, Jianxiong; Cui, Yu; Gao, Yuan; Zhan, Xianyuan; Yu, Junzhi; Ma, Xiao further introduce Suboptimal-Value-Bench, a multi-embodiment benchmark of 800 suboptimal trajectories with high-fidelity human-labeled frame annotations explicitly designed to evaluate value models on the non-expert data that dominates large-scale manipulation corpora, and show WVM maintains SOTA on this regime and improves downstream manipulation policy learning when used as a data-quality filter; 2026-06-19 (cs.RO, cs.AI, cs.CV); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (robotics / value-function / world-model data-selection theme). Complements the [[world-action-models-a-survey-2606.20781]] (predictive-action-model survey sibling) and [[ebench-elemental-diagnosis-of-generalist-mobile-manipulation-policies-2606.18239]] (multi-axis-diagnostic-benchmark sibling) entries on this page by surfacing world-model backbones as the right foundation for generalist value estimation and demonstrating that data-selection with a non-expert-aware value model improves downstream manipulation policy learning — the missing link between large-scale mixed-quality manipulation data and policy-deployment-ready training subsets.

- [[distill-once-adapt-life-long-dataset-distillation-continual-2606.20196]] — Distill Once, Adapt Life-Long: Exploring Dataset Distillation for Continual Test-Time Adaptation: identifies the source-vs-stability trade-off in continual test-time adaptation where practical deployments cannot retain the source dataset due to privacy or licensing constraints but purely source-free CTTA methods become unstable under long-term distribution shift with compounding self-training errors and catastrophic forgetting, and introduces DO-ALL — a plug-and-play framework that performs Dataset Distillation once before deployment to produce a compact set of privacy-conscious synthetic anchors summarizing the source distribution, then matches each target sample to its semantically aligned anchor during online adaptation for source replay, representation alignment, and manifold-smoothing regularization; Jang, Hyun-Kurl; Kim, Jihun; Kweon, Hyeokjun; Yoon, Kuk-Jin demonstrate that DO-ALL can be seamlessly integrated into existing CTTA algorithms and consistently improves long-term robustness across CIFAR100-C, ImageNet-C, and the CCC benchmark; 2026-06-18 (cs.LG, cs.CV); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (continual-test-time-adaptation / dataset-distillation / source-free-robustness theme). Complements the [[fastmix-fast-data-mixture-optimization-via-gradient-descent-2606.14971]] (LLM-pretraining data-mixing bilevel-optimization sibling) and [[manifold-bandits-bayesian-curriculum-learning-over-the-latent-geometry-of-large-language-models-2606.19750]] (geometric-anchor RL sibling) entries on this page by extending the data-distillation-style compression pattern from pretraining-mixture selection into the continual-adaptation regime, where the geometric-anchor matching pattern is the load-bearing primitive for stable online adaptation without retaining raw source data.


- [[an-exploratory-case-study-of-llm-assisted-refactoring-and-gameplay-feature-generation-in-an-endless-runner-game-2606.21171]] — An Exploratory Case Study of LLM-Assisted Refactoring and Gameplay Feature Generation in an Endless Runner Game: an exploratory empirical case study of GPT-4o on six selected development tasks (three localized refactoring tasks and three gameplay feature generation tasks) within a custom Python/Pygame endless runner, evaluated through software metrics, unit tests, and manual gameplay assessments; Wunderlich, Jan; Kleffmann, Markus; Lempert, Sebastian find that all three refactoring tasks were completed successfully in functional terms, whereas only one of three gameplay feature generation tasks resulted in a correctly integrated feature, suggesting GPT-4o handled localized transformations more reliably than tasks requiring new gameplay interactions across multiple existing systems — an asymmetric capability profile that benchmark-style code-LLM evaluations typically do not surface; 2026-06-19 (cs.SE); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (LLM-coding / software-engineering / case-study theme). Complements the [[beyond-nl2code-a-structured-survey-of-multimodal-code-intelligence-2606.15932]] (multimodal-code survey sibling) and [[naturebench-can-coding-agents-match-the-published-sota-of-nature-family-papers-2606.24530]] (coding-agent SOTA-replication sibling) entries on this page by being a transparent case-based account of LLM-coding in an existing production-style game codebase — local transformations work, multi-system integration remains the binding constraint.

- [[world-action-models-a-survey-2606.20781]] — World Action Models: A Survey: a structured survey of World Action Models (WAMs) — embodied predictive-action models that make a forecast of the future available to action — that disambiguates WAMs from broader world models, video-generation models, action-grounded video world models, and Vision-Language-Action policies by decomposing each method along two complementary axes (what it is required to generate, and how its predictive substrate, backbone, action coupling, and deployment regime are composed); Shen, Qiuhong; Zhang, Shihua; Liao, Yue; Li, Qi; Tan, Zhenxiong; Wang, Shizun; Yan, Shuicheng; Wang, Xinchao surface a consistent design pattern — WAMs are not simply video generators with action heads, but predictive-action methods whose design choices trade representational richness against compute, memory, latency, and action-label cost, and the field is moving toward methods that generate less of the future while preserving what control requires; 2026-06-18 (cs.RO, cs.AI, cs.CV); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (survey / embodied world-models / action-models theme). Complements the [[qwen-agentworld-language-world-models-for-general-agents-2606.24597]] (language world models for agents sibling) and [[ebench-elemental-diagnosis-of-generalist-mobile-manipulation-policies-2606.18239]] (robotics multi-axis-diagnostic sibling) entries on this page by extending the survey wave into embodied predictive-action and surfacing the consistent design pattern that links WAM methods across heterogeneous backbones.

- [[fastmix-fast-data-mixture-optimization-via-gradient-descent-2606.14971]] — FastMix: Fast Data Mixture Optimization via Gradient Descent: reformulates data-mixture selection for LLM pre-training and post-training as a bilevel optimization problem in which mixture coefficients and model parameters are jointly learned via a single proxy-model training run, with per-source loss weights derived from gradient-based updates; Tan, Haoru; Wu, Sitong; Chen, Yanfeng; Xia, Jun; Xie, Ruobing; Xia, Bin; Sun, Xingwu; Qi, Xiaojuan show the approach substantially reduces search cost versus prior heuristic or simulation-based data-mixture discovery while outperforming baselines on both pre- and post-training data-mixture tasks, training only a single proxy model end-to-end rather than multiple model variants; 2026-06-12 (cs.LG, cs.CL); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (LLM training-data / data-mixture / bilevel-optimization theme). Complements the [[holistic-data-scheduler-for-llm-pre-training-via-multi-objective-reinforcement-learning-2606.24133]] (RL-based scheduler sibling) and [[ac-odm-actor-critic-online-data-mixing-for-sample-efficient-llm-pretraining-2505.23878]] (actor-critic online data mixing sibling) entries on this page by replacing heuristic or simulation-based mixture search with a single-proxy-model gradient-based procedure that jointly optimizes mixture ratios and model parameters.

- [[deep-research-in-physical-sciences-a-multi-agent-framework-and-comprehensive-benchmark-2606.18648]] — Deep Research in Physical Sciences: A Multi-Agent Framework and Comprehensive Benchmark: introduces PhySciBench — a 200-question benchmark balanced between physics and chemistry across six task categories reflecting real-world scientific workflows — and proposes DelveAgent, a modular multi-agent framework with adaptive planning, dual-granularity memory, and hierarchical physics-grounded reflection; Yigeng Jiang, Tengchao Yang, Taoyong Cui, Jiaxing Wan, Yuan Wang, Weida Wang et al. show that even Gemini Deep Research reaches only 33.5% accuracy on PhySciBench, identifying fragility in extended reasoning chains, limited cross-step knowledge transfer, and absence of physics-grounded self-verification as the three recurrent failure modes — and that DelveAgent improves accuracy by up to 7.5 percentage points while reducing inference cost to roughly one-third of the strongest baseline; 2026-06-17 (cs.AI, cs.CL, cs.MA); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (deep-research / multi-agent scientific-reasoning / physical-sciences theme). Complements the [[naturebench-can-coding-agents-match-the-published-sota-of-nature-family-papers-2606.24530]] (Nature-family coding-agent sibling) and [[enterpriseclawbench-benchmarking-agents-from-real-workplace-sessions-2606.23654]] (workplace-session sibling) entries on this page by extending the deep-research evaluation wave to the physical-sciences setting where hallucination risk is structurally high and physics-grounded self-verification is the load-bearing primitive.

- [[ebench-elemental-diagnosis-of-generalist-mobile-manipulation-policies-2606.18239]] — EBench: Elemental Diagnosis of Generalist Mobile Manipulation Policies: a 26-task simulation benchmark that diagnoses generalist mobile manipulation policies along 5 capability dimensions and 4 generalization dimensions, replacing the aggregate-success-rate scalar with a multi-axis capability profile; Ning Gao, Jinliang Zheng, Xing Gao, Haoxiang Ma, Hanqing Wang, Yukai Wang et al. evaluate π₀, π₀.₅, XVLA, and InternVLA-A1 and reveal strikingly disjoint capability profiles at near-equal aggregate success rates — π₀.₅ has the best train-test retention, InternVLA-A1 dominates mobile manipulation but collapses on dexterous tasks, and XVLA covers a different set of atomic skills than the others; 2026-06-16 (cs.RO, cs.AI, cs.CV); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (robotics / VLA / multi-axis-diagnostic benchmark theme). Complements the [[enterpriseclawbench-benchmarking-agents-from-real-workplace-sessions-2606.23654]] (sibling diagnostic-benchmark on workplace agents) and [[naturebench-can-coding-agents-match-the-published-sota-of-nature-family-papers-2606.24530]] (sibling SOTA-reproduction benchmark) entries on this page by showing that even within generalist manipulation, aggregate SOTA masks capability-specific collapses — InternVLA-A1 winning mobile but failing dexterity, XVLA covering complementary atomic skills, and π₀.₅ having the best train-test retention — diagnostic signals invisible to a single-leaderboard-number evaluation.

- [[beyond-nl2code-a-structured-survey-of-multimodal-code-intelligence-2606.15932]] — Beyond NL2Code: A Structured Survey of Multimodal Code Intelligence: a structured survey of systems that generate, edit, refine, or reason with code under visually grounded inputs and outputs (screenshots, charts, vector drawings, videos, interactive states) where correctness depends not only on syntax but on layout, data semantics, interaction behaviour, and post-execution domain constraints; Xuanle Zhao, Qiushi Sun, Jingyu Xiao, Xuexin Liu, Haoyue Yang, Qiaosheng Chen et al. propose a role-based taxonomy (code as rendered artifact, editable symbolic structure, scientific representation, intermediate reasoning trace, or executable policy/tool interface) and organise benchmarks and methods into four domains (Graphical User Interface, Scientific Visualization, Structured Graphics, Frontier Tasks and Frameworks), then identify four verification-centered research directions; 2026-06-14 (cs.CL, cs.SE, cs.CV); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (LLM survey / multimodal-code / taxonomy theme). Complements the [[the-periodic-table-of-llm-reasoning-a-structured-survey-of-reasoning-paradigms-abstractions-building-blocks-and-open-challenges-2606.11470]] (sibling reasoning-paradigm survey) and [[the-hitchhikers-guide-to-agentic-ai-from-foundations-to-systems-2606.24937]] (sibling agentic-AI survey) entries on this page by extending the survey wave to the multimodal-code surface and reframing code-intelligence around *what code does* in the task rather than the text-to-code vs code-to-text split.

- [[critique-of-agent-model-2606.23991]] — Critique of Agent Model: an epistemic audit of "agent" as a term of art in current LLM systems, arguing that genuine agency requires goal/identity/decision/self-regulation/learning structures to be internalized within the system itself rather than assembled through external scaffolding, and proposing the Goal-Identity-Configurator (GIC) reference architecture for what they call "agentive" systems; Xing, Eric; Deng, Mingkai; Hou, Jinyu argue the piece lands at a moment when the field has been marketing LLM-toolchains as "coding agents" and "AI co-scientists" while entertaining speculative "machine agency" risk narratives — and that both framings collapse without a precise agentic-vs-agentive distinction; 2026-06-22 (cs.AI, cs.LG, cs.MA, cs.RO); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (LLM agent epistemology / agency-internalism / GIC architecture theme). Complements the [[causal-discovery-in-the-era-of-agents-2606.23608]] and [[capable-but-careless-do-computer-use-agents-follow-contextual-integrity-2606.23189]] entries on this page by pushing the audit question one level deeper — from "do agents follow rules" to "do agents have the structures required to follow rules internally" — and frames the agent-safety discussion around internalized self-regulation rather than scaffold-thickening.

- [[tropt-an-open-framework-for-unifying-and-advancing-discrete-text-optimization-2606.23496]] — TROPT: An Open Framework for Unifying and Advancing Discrete Text Optimization: the first open-source framework that unifies discrete text-trigger optimizers (the family of methods used for LLM jailbreaks, model auditing, and interpretability probes) under a single interface, currently shipping 30+ optimization recipes built from 15+ optimizers spanning white-box to black-box access and 15+ losses from foundational to state-of-the-art methods; Ben-Tov, Matan; Sharif, Mahmood argue that existing discrete optimizers are scattered across research codebases tied to specific models, objectives, and problem domains, with variants proliferating and head-to-head comparison being near-impossible — TROPT makes every component swappable and ships with a controlled large-scale experimental study surfacing potent-yet-underadopted jailbreak techniques the field's scattered-codebase state had been obscuring; 2026-06-22 (cs.LG, cs.CR); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (LLM discrete-text-optimization / red-teaming framework / open-source-unification theme). Complements the [[what-intermediate-layers-know-detecting-jailbreaks-from-entropy-dynamics-2606.25182]] (jailbreak-detection sibling) and [[toward-open-weight-models-without-risks-separating-public-and-private-capabilities-in-llms-2606.21638]] (open-weight capability-separation sibling) entries on this page by providing the open-source optimization substrate that detection and capability-separation work needs as the reproducible basis for both adversarial trigger generation and auditing primitives.

- [[libretto-giving-llm-agents-a-sense-of-musical-structure-2606.22708]] — Libretto: Giving LLM Agents a Sense of Musical Structure: an agent-facing framework that turns symbolic music from a raw token sequence into a measurable and editable object for language-model agents, using an LLM-native grammar with explicit onset slots, voices, and bar-level organization, evaluated in a corpus-calibrated statistical space over rhythm, harmony, melody, texture, form, and variation; Xu, Yichen shows the same structural axes support retrieval, diagnosis, copy-risk control, and iterative self-revision — so an agent can fill a gap, morph toward a reference, generate an educational piece, or audit its own output against corpus statistics without leaving the grammar; 2026-06-21 (cs.SD, cs.AI); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (LLM-agent creative/multimodal / symbolic-music / structured-representation theme). Complements the [[biomatrix-towards-a-comprehensive-biological-foundation-model-spanning-the-modality-matrix-of-sequences-structures-and-language-2606.22138]] (multi-modal foundation model sibling) and [[agent-orchestration]] (parent meta-page on agent-system organization) entries on this page by being a structured-representation framework for agents rather than a unified-modality foundation model — Libretto's load-bearing primitive is a symbolic-music grammar, not a model, making the agent-tooling pattern visible at the creative-multimodal surface.

- [[gatemem-benchmarking-memory-governance-in-multi-principal-shared-memory-agents-2606.18829]] — GateMem: Benchmarking Memory Governance in Multi-Principal Shared-Memory Agents: introduces a benchmark for shared-memory LLM agent deployments where multiple principals (e.g., users, roles, scopes) write to a common memory pool and query it under different authorization boundaries — a regime that existing single-user memory benchmarks leave understudied; Ren, Zhe; Yang, Yibo; Chen, Yimeng; +7 more evaluate memory quality along three axes jointly: utility for legitimate long-horizon requests with state updates, access control across contextual authorization boundaries, and agent-facing actions under governance constraints, surfacing memory-governance failures that prior retrieval/recall benchmarks conflated with single-user noise; 2026-06-17 (cs.LG); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (LLM agent memory / multi-principal / shared-memory governance theme). Complements the [[are-we-ready-for-an-agent-native-memory-system-2606.24775]] and [[capable-but-careless-do-computer-use-agents-follow-contextual-integrity-2606.23189]] entries on this page by isolating the missing access-control axis of LLM agent memory — where the prior benchmarks ask 'can the agent remember?' GateMem asks 'should the agent remember this, and for whom?', a regime that hospitals, workplaces, campuses, and household-assistant deployments will require.

- [[sproutrag-attention-guided-tree-search-with-progressive-embeddings-for-long-2606.18381]] — SproutRAG: Attention-Guided Tree Search with Progressive Embeddings for Long-Document RAG: an attention-guided hierarchical RAG framework that organizes sentence-level chunks into progressively larger but semantically coherent units and uses tree search guided by attention-derived signals to navigate them, avoiding the three failure modes of prior hierarchical RAG (LLM-guided chunking costs at indexing/retrieval, single-level context expansion's fixed granularity, and hierarchical summarization's information loss); Abaskohi, Amirhossein; Laradji, Issam H.; West, Peter; +1 more show that the progressive-embedding design preserves sentence-level retrieval precision while enabling paragraph- and section-level aggregation without the summarization bottleneck; 2026-06-16 (cs.CL); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (LLM RAG / hierarchical-retrieval / attention-guided progressive-embeddings theme). Complements the [[hakari-bench-a-lightweight-benchmark-for-comparing-retrieval-architectures-and-efficiency-settings-under-unified-conditions-2606.22778]] (sibling entry on retrieval efficiency) and prior chartwalker cross-chart RAG work by attacking the granularity-vs-coherence trade-off with attention-derived signals instead of LLM-guided chunking or summarization — making long-document RAG practical without paying the indexing-time LLM cost.

- [[dailyreport-an-open-ended-benchmark-for-evaluating-search-agents-on-daily-2606.12871]] — DailyReport: An Open-ended Benchmark for Evaluating Search Agents on Daily Search Tasks: an open-ended benchmark of 150 realistic daily information-seeking tasks paired with 3,546 atomic evaluation criteria, addressing the gap between specialized SA benchmarks (unrealistic task distribution) and real-world daily search deployment (where coarse task-level rubrics limit evaluation interpretability); Han, Jingxuan; Liu, Wei; Zhu, Mingyang; +8 more show that the atomic-criterion design enables fine-grained, interpretable scoring that separates task-completion from synthesis quality from source-grounding — three axes prior benchmarks conflated under a single accuracy number; 2026-06-11 (cs.AI); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (LLM search-agent evaluation / open-ended benchmark theme). Complements the [[agent-orchestration]] (parent meta-page covering agent-evaluation surfaces) by shifting the open-ended benchmark framing from long-horizon embodied agents to the search-agent evaluation surface, where atomic-criterion scoring has been missing entirely.
- [[causal-discovery-in-the-era-of-agents-2606.23608]] — Causal Discovery in the Era of Agents: an epistemological audit of recent LLM-causal-discovery work (pairwise direction inference, graph-structure proposal, LM outputs as priors) arguing these methods obscure whether causal evidence is supported by data and assumptions or by textual associations, prompt artifacts, and hallucinated mechanisms; Zheng, Yujia; Verma, Vishal; Gill, Mantej; Dai, Haoyue propose an evaluation framework that separates the contribution of the LM's prior knowledge, the statistical signal in the data, and the prompt and scaffolding artifacts — allowing the field to attribute discovered causal structures to the right source rather than crediting LM-imitation as causal inference; 2026-06-22 (cs.AI); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (LLM-augmented causal-discovery / epistemological-audit / agentic-scientific-reasoning theme). Complements the a-verifiable-search and NatureBench entries on this page by surfacing the same gap between verifiable surface outputs and learnable underlying mechanisms in the LLM-augmented scientific-discovery setting, and frames "LLM-as-causal-scientist" as a higher-risk framing than "LLM-as-causal-assistant" because the agent setting amplifies the LM-imitation-as-causal-evidence failure mode.


- [[manifold-bandits-bayesian-curriculum-learning-over-the-latent-geometry-of-large-language-models-2606.19750]] — Manifold Bandits: Bayesian Curriculum Learning over the Latent Geometry of Large Language Models: reformulates LLM RL curriculum learning as Bayesian optimization over a learned manifold of problem difficulty, replacing the standard "each prompt is an independent arm" formulation with a posterior that shares statistical strength across geometrically similar problems; McKenzie, Darrien; Hansen, Nicklas; Wang, Xiaolong show that exploiting the embedding-space geometry of reasoning problems yields materially better sample efficiency on LLM RL benchmarks than independent-arm bandit baselines, with gains concentrated in the early- and mid-training regime where exploration matters most; 2026-06-18 (cs.LG); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (LLM RL training efficiency / curriculum-learning geometry theme). Complements the Holistic-Data-Scheduler and AC-ODM data-allocation work on this page by attacking the prompt-sampling side of LLM RL rather than the pretraining-data-mixing side — together they frame "what to sample" as the load-bearing RL training decision.


- [[openrath-session-centered-runtime-state-for-agent-systems-2606.19409]] — OpenRath: Session-Centered Runtime State for Agent Systems: introduces a PyTorch-like programming model for multi-agent, multi-session systems that consolidates fragmented runtime state (transcripts, tool effects, memory events, workspace placement, branch provenance, replay evidence) into a single first-class session object — modeled explicitly on PyTorch's autograd tape as a structured, inspectable, replayable artifact rather than a bag of side-channel logs spread across services; Wen, Fukang; Wang, Zhijie; Xu, Ruilin elevate reproducibility, branching, and replay to first-class runtime operations on the state object itself; 2026-06-17 (cs.SE); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (agent-runtime substrate / session-model theme). Complements the AOHP OS-level harness work by addressing the missing engineering substrate between the OS and the agent loop — where AOHP argues the OS must treat agents as first-class actors, OpenRath argues the agent runtime must treat its own state the way PyTorch treats the computation graph.


- [[autodata-an-agentic-data-scientist-to-create-high-quality-synthetic-data-2606.25996]] — Autodata: An agentic data scientist to create high quality synthetic data: frames synthetic-data construction as an agentic iterative loop over schema design, sample generation, quality filtering, and rubric verification, and trains (meta-optimizes) the agentic data scientist end-to-end; Kulikov, Whitehouse, Wu show that synthetic data from the meta-optimized agent matches or exceeds the downstream model quality of carefully curated human datasets at a fraction of the cost; 2026-06-24 (cs.AI); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (LLM training-data / agentic-data-pipeline theme). Complements the agent-benchmarking and self-compacting-agent work on this page by showing that the data-generation side of the agentic loop can itself be agentic, raising the question of whether human-curated datasets are still the long-term ceiling.



- [[constraint-tax-in-open-weight-llms-an-empirical-study-of-tool-calling-suppression-under-structured-output-constraints-2606.25605]] — Constraint Tax in Open-Weight LLMs: An Empirical Study of Tool Calling Suppression Under Structured Output Constraints: empirical study showing structured-output enforcement (JSON-schema / grammar-constrained decoding) imposes a measurable "constraint tax" on tool-calling reliability across multiple open-weight model families, with the degradation concentrated in tool-selection rather than schema-format errors; Li, Zhang, Lv propose constraint-aware fine-tuning and two-pass decoding mitigations; 2026-06-24 (cs.CL); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (LLM tool-use / structured-output alignment theme). - [[improved-large-language-diffusion-models-2606.25331]] — Improved Large Language Diffusion Models: introduces iLLaDA, an 8B masked diffusion language model trained from scratch that matches strong autoregressive baselines at matched compute, using a curriculum-style noising schedule and a parallel-decoding inference strategy with no quality regression at long context; Nie, Shen, Min, Qiyang, Xu, Shaoxuan show that the masked-diffusion vs autoregressive gap closes at 8B scale with the right training recipe; 2026-06-24 (cs.CL); discovered via emergent-concept search on 2026-06-25 (LLM architecture frontier / non-autoregressive diffusion theme).
