# Self-Improvement of Large Language Models: A Technical Overview and Future Outlook

## Overview
A comprehensive technical survey of LLM self-improvement methods, covering the taxonomy of approaches (self-supervised refinement, self-rewarding, self-verification, evolutionary search, and reflective reasoning), the theoretical limits of self-improvement, and a roadmap of open challenges. Serves as the foundational survey for the wiki's self-improvement / strategy-memory / flow-reasoning theme axis.

## Key Facts
- **Authors**: Yang, Haoyan; Xerri, Mario; Park, Solha + 4 others
- **Year**: 2026
- **arXiv**: [2603.25681](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25681)

## Key Contributions
- Unified taxonomy of LLM self-improvement paradigms: self-evolution, self-training, self-rewarding, self-verification, and reflective reasoning
- Formal analysis of scaling laws and diminishing returns in self-improvement without external feedback
- Survey of 40+ self-improvement methods with empirical comparison across reasoning benchmarks
- Identifies symbolic model synthesis as a critical missing component for breaking self-improvement ceilings
- Open challenges: hallucination accumulation, catastrophic forgetting in self-generated data, evaluation benchmark saturation
