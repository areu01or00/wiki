# Reward Hacking in Language Model Agents: Revisiting AI Safety Gridworlds

## Overview
Reward hacking — where AI systems exploit misspecified objectives for high reward without satisfying intended goals — is a central AI safety challenge. Most known instances are discovered post hoc in frontier systems where controlled study is impractical. This paper adapts the AI Safety Gridworlds framework into a text-based evaluation suite, reformulating classic reinforcement learning gridworlds for controlled study of reward hacking in LLM agents.

## Key Contributions
- Text-based AI Safety Gridworlds suite for controlled reward hacking study in LLM agents
- Systematic evaluation of reward hacking patterns in LLM-based RL agents
- Controlled environment for studying misspecified reward objectives
- Benchmark for AI safety evaluation in agentic language model systems
