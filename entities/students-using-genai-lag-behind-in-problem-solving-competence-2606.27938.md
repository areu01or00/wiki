# Students using GenAI lag behind in problem-solving competence: an agent-based study of classroom networks

## Overview
Uses agent-based modeling to study how GenAI support affects problem-solving competence (PSC) development in high school physics classrooms. Simulates student networks with individual, collaborative, and GenAI-supported task completion. Finds that GenAI use diminishes collective competence development and increases the share of students remaining in lower competence tiers — suggesting educational GenAI impact must be assessed at the collective level, not just individual outcomes.

## Key Contributions
- First agent-based model of GenAI's collective impact on classroom competence dynamics
- Shows GenAI use widens lower-competence tier share across peer-network structures
- Demonstrates cognitive offloading to GenAI eliminates necessary deep learning friction
- Collective outcomes perspective challenges individual-level GenAI learning studies
- Peer-network structure modulates GenAI's negative competence effects
